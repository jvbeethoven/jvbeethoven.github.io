Background

At one of the companies I worked for, we decided to create a design system. Big buzzword alert here, but there was an actual underlying reason why we decided to build a small scale system.

  • There was a lot of chaos in the applications that we worked on;
  • We were building multiple big platforms, of which the design was inconsistent;
  • The design being implemented had no proper guidance and resulted in bad user experience
  • The UI/component library needed a big update;
  • There was no real design knowledge in the company so it would be a good opportunity to educate everyone on making good design choices.

One other designer and I were given some time spend on creating a system that would tackle the issues mentioned above. Below you can read more about our proces.

Result and retrospective

Working on a design system has been quite challenging and interesting for me personally. However, there have been a lot of things that I learned through failure whilst working on this project.

First of all, this was my real first job after having graduated. There wasn't any established design process in the company and I felt that I needed a design mentor. After struggling for a bit more than one year, I've eventually decided to leave the company as I wanted to learn from others first before tackling such an impactful project.
